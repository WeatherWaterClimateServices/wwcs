The **Weather-Water-Climate Services (WWCS)** initiative was established to strengthen the resilience and well-being of rural communities in Tajikistan through improved access to localized weather, water, and climate information systems. WWCS was jointly financed by Caritas Switzerland and the Swiss Government, supporting the development of a growing network of low-cost environmental monitoring systems and associated operational tools across Tajikistan and Uzbekistan. Initially developed and tested through localized implementation in the district of Lakhsh, as a proof of concept, the initiative has since evolved into a mature operational platform supporting climate adaptation, environmental monitoring, and advisory services applicable for multiple regions and application areas.

Under the lead of Caritas Switzerland, the open-source resources contained within this repository are being further refined, expanded, and operationalized through collaboration with like-minded partners such as *Rizome Systems*, an initiative of Caritas Switzerland. This includes continued development of low-cost sensing systems, advisory services, environmental monitoring applications, and scalable deployment models designed for adaptation across diverse geographic and institutional contexts.

The initiative continues to emphasize transparency, local ownership, interoperability, and practical implementation through open architectures that support long-term sustainability and broad accessibility.
